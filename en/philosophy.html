Philosophy

KAPT is a young architecture agency founded by Anas Koubaiti and Théo Pagnon. Building on their respective experiences as project managers for almost a decade each, at international architecture agencies (Richard Rogers, Studio Odile Decq, Agence Ter), they decided to work together on a range of scales, from urban projects to space and furniture design. They also collaborate in an academic context, teaching together at an architecture school since 2020. KAPT designs innovative, aesthetic, sustainable, and concrete architecture, creatively solving the constraints and challenges faced by its clients. The concern to offer flexible spaces separated from service facilities makes the buildings designed adaptable and resilient in a world where technology is rapidly evolving. A profound belief in the importance of the sustainability of buildings underlies the agency's work.

Method

As both architects and engineers, Anas and Théo strive to formulate a holistic vision of their architectural practice. Their double culture, technical and artistic, leads them to formulate bold, poetic, and yet sober proposals, rooted in their context and in harmony with their time. Recent projects such as the family home in Rabat, Morocco or the vacation villa in Santa Teresa, Costa Rica, illustrate this vision through various environmental characteristics integrated into the DNA of the buildings. Particular attention is paid to details throughout the design process. Based in Paris, KAPT works on projects with varying horizons, both in France and internationally, and the experience gained from working with great names in architecture as well as proven work rigor allows them to approach projects with the confidence necessary for the successful completion of ambitious projects.

Biographies

Anas Koubaiti

Architect – Engineer – Urbanist

A dual graduate of the École Nationale Supérieure d'Architecture de Paris-La Villette and ESTP Paris, Anas is also trained in Urban Planning from the Urban School at Sciences Po. Following experiences in Milan, Geneva and Hanoï, Anas joined Studio Odil Decq in 2015. Being based in Paris, he has been taking on numerous projects at varying scales, ranging from artistic installation creation and major urban projects. Since 2014, he has been associated with the art and essay publication Whitelies Magazine. Anas continues to actively influence the editorial line with creative content as an artistic director. In 2020, Anas contributed to the strategic development plan of Al Ula Oasis in Saudi Arabia with Agence Ter and is involved in Academia at the Architecture School of the International University of Rabat.

Theo Pagnon

Engineer – Architect

Theo is trained as an engineer and architect. He studied a dual degree course at the Special school of civil engineering in Paris and the National School of Architecture in Paris la Villette and works since 2013 at Rogers Stirk Harbour + Partners (RSHP), an international architecture practice based in London. Theo supervised the work on the Louvre Conservation Centre site for two years. He is now responsible for the development of the Parisian office of RSHP since January 2020 by striving to take advantage of this dual culture, both pragmatic and creative. Theo has worked on many projects from the metropolitan scale to mastering of execution details. Thanks to his dual training, this structure and systems enthusiast brings a holistic and rigorous approach to his practice of architecture. Theo is also a big theatre lover, being an actor himself in his spare time. Finally, he is a cycling enthusiast whose bicycle collection seriously clutters his Parisian apartment.
